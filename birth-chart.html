What's in a Vedic Birth Chart

A Vedic birth chart (Janam Kundli) is a map of the sky at the exact moment and location of your birth. It captures the positions of all 9 Vedic planets across the 12 zodiac signs and 12 houses, forming the foundation of every Jyotish prediction about your life.

The Key Components

Ascendant (Lagna)

The zodiac sign rising on the eastern horizon at your birth time. The Ascendant determines the layout of all 12 houses in your chart and strongly influences your personality, physical appearance, and life approach. It is the most time-sensitive element — even a few minutes can change it.

9 Planets (Navagraha)

Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, and Ketu. Each planet governs specific life areas and carries unique energy. Their sign placements, house positions, and mutual aspects create the unique blueprint of your life.

12 Houses (Bhavas)

Each house governs a specific domain of life — from personality (1st) to finances (2nd), communication (3rd), home (4th), creativity (5th), health (6th), marriage (7th), transformation (8th), fortune (9th), career (10th), gains (11th), to spirituality and losses (12th).

Aspects (Drishti)

In Vedic astrology, planets cast aspects on other houses and planets. Unlike Western astrology where aspects are mutual, Vedic aspects are directional. Jupiter, Mars, and Saturn have special aspects that extend their influence across multiple houses, creating additional connections in your chart.

Rashi Chart vs Navamsa Chart

Rashi Chart (D1)

  • Main birth chart — your life blueprint
  • Shows planetary positions in 12 signs
  • Reveals overall personality & life themes
  • Used for career, health, general predictions
  • Each sign spans 30 degrees

Navamsa Chart (D9)

  • Divisional chart — deeper planetary strength
  • Divides each sign into 9 parts (3°20')
  • Key for marriage & spouse analysis
  • Shows spiritual evolution & dharma
  • Confirms or modifies D1 predictions

A planet that is strong in the Rashi chart but weak in the Navamsa may not deliver its full promise. Conversely, a planet weak in D1 but strong in D9 can still produce good results over time. Classical Vedic astrologers always read both charts together — and so does Om.AI.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Vedic birth chart (Janam Kundli)?

A Vedic birth chart or Janam Kundli is a map of the sky at the exact moment of your birth, as seen from your birthplace. It shows the positions of all 9 Vedic planets across the 12 houses and 12 zodiac signs. The chart is the foundation of all Vedic astrology predictions — your personality, career, relationships, health, and life timing are all read from it.

What information do I need to generate my birth chart?

You need three pieces of information: your date of birth, exact time of birth, and place of birth. The time of birth is especially critical — it determines your Ascendant (Lagna), house cusps, and Moon Nakshatra. Even a few minutes difference can change the Ascendant sign, which alters the entire chart interpretation. If you don't know your exact birth time, check your birth certificate or hospital records.

What is the difference between Rashi chart and Navamsa chart?

The Rashi chart (D1) is your main birth chart showing planetary positions in the 12 signs and houses. It reveals your overall life blueprint. The Navamsa chart (D9) divides each sign into 9 parts (3 degrees 20 minutes each) and is used to assess the deeper strength of planets, marriage prospects, and spiritual evolution. A planet strong in the Rashi chart but weak in Navamsa may not deliver its full promise. Vedic astrologers always read both charts together.

What are yogas and doshas in a birth chart?

Yogas are specific planetary combinations that indicate good fortune or special abilities — like Rajayoga (power and success), Dhana Yoga (wealth), or Gajakesari Yoga (wisdom and fame). Doshas are challenging combinations — like Mangal Dosha (Mars affliction affecting marriage) or Kaal Sarpa Dosha (all planets between Rahu-Ketu axis).
