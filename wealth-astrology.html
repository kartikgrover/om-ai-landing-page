How AI Reads Your Wealth Chart

Vedic astrology (Jyotish) treats wealth as a multi-house phenomenon — money is earned, accumulated, gained, inherited, and spent through different houses. The AI reads all of these together rather than looking at one factor in isolation.

2nd House

Accumulated wealth, savings, family money, and your relationship with material resources.

11th House

Gains, income, network, and the fulfillment of material desires — the most direct house of earning.

5th House

Speculation, investments, and purva punya — past-life merit that traditionally fuels lucky financial breaks.

9th House

Fortune, blessings, dharma, and inheritance — the broader luck factor that supports lasting prosperity.

Jupiter (Guru)

Natural significator of wealth and wisdom-driven expansion. Its strength shapes how money grows.

Venus (Shukra)

Significator of luxury, comfort, and Lakshmi-style prosperity. Strong Venus indicates ease with money.

Mercury (Budha)

Trade, commerce, calculation, and business intelligence — the planet behind entrepreneurial wealth.

Moon (Chandra)

Cash flow, liquidity, and the public-facing demand for what you offer. Strong Moon supports steady income.

Dhana Yogas Explained

Classical texts such as Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra, Phaladeepika, and Uttara Kalamrita describe specific planetary combinations called Dhana Yogas — wealth combinations. They are formed when the lords of wealth-related houses connect through conjunction, mutual aspect, or sign exchange (parivartana).

2nd–11th Lord Dhana Yoga

Conjunction, mutual aspect, or exchange between the 2nd and 11th lords. Traditionally tied to steady earning combined with the ability to save and accumulate.

5th–9th Lord Dhana Yoga

Connection between the 5th (purva punya) and 9th (fortune) lords. Often associated with wealth that arrives through luck, inheritance, blessings, or speculative gains.

Lakshmi Yoga

The 9th lord placed in a Kendra (1st, 4th, 7th, 10th) in its own sign or exaltation, with a strong Lagna lord. Traditionally associated with lasting, dignified prosperity.

Gaja-Kesari Yoga

Jupiter in a Kendra from the Moon. A general benefic combination linked to good standing, sustained income, and wisdom-supported financial decisions.

Chandra-Mangala Yoga

Conjunction of Moon and Mars. Classical texts associate it with the capacity to convert effort into earnings, often through trade, real estate, or active enterprise.

Dhana-Karaka Jupiter Combinations

Jupiter conjoined with or aspecting wealth-house lords. As the natural karaka of Dhana, a well-placed Jupiter strengthens almost every other wealth signature in the chart.

Daridra Yogas — and how to read them

Classical texts also describe Daridra Yogas — combinations that may obstruct wealth, such as the 11th lord placed in the 6th, 8th, or 12th house, or the 2nd lord heavily afflicted by malefics. These are not deterministic. A chart with one Daridra Yoga can still produce wealth if other Dhana Yogas, a strong Lagna lord, or favorable Dashas are present. Om.AI weighs both sides rather than reading any single combination as a final verdict.

Dasha-based wealth windows

A Dhana Yoga is a promise in the chart. The Vimshottari Dasha system tells you when that promise activates. Wealth events tend to appear during the Mahadasha or Antardasha of planets that form the yoga, or of the 2nd or 11th lord. Om.AI maps your full Dasha timeline and flags the periods most likely to switch a wealth combination on.

Traditional Remedies for Wealth Houses

Classical texts including Lal Kitab and Phaladeepika describe simple remedial practices when wealth-related planets are weak or afflicted. These are supportive spiritual practices, not guaranteed outcomes.

Lakshmi Mantra

Devotional chanting of the Mahalakshmi mantra (Om Shreem Mahalakshmiyei Namaha) is a traditional Friday practice associated with Venus and Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of abundance.

Gemstones

Yellow sapphire (Jupiter), diamond or white sapphire (Venus), and emerald (Mercury) are traditionally suggested when those planets are well-placed but weak by strength. Always consult a qualified astrologer before wearing a gemstone.

Donations (Daan)

Donations during the Dasha of an afflicted planet — such as yellow items for Jupiter, white items for Venus, or feeding the needy on Saturdays for Saturn — are a traditional way to soften malefic influences.

Lal Kitab Practices

Lal Kitab offers simple household remedies — such as keeping a silver vessel, planting specific trees, or feeding cows — tied to specific planetary placements in wealth houses.

What is a Dhana Yoga?

Dhana Yoga is a category of planetary combinations in Vedic astrology traditionally associated with wealth. It typically forms when the lords of wealth-related houses — the 2nd, 5th, 9th, and 11th — connect through conjunction, mutual aspect, or sign exchange. Classical texts such as Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra and Phaladeepika describe several variations, including the 2nd–11th lord Dhana Yoga and the 5th–9th lord Dhana Yoga. Read the full Dhana Yoga guide.

Which houses indicate wealth in Vedic astrology?

The 2nd house represents accumulated wealth, savings, and family money. The 11th house represents gains, income, and the fulfillment of desires. The 5th house represents speculative gains and purva punya (past-life merit). The 9th house represents fortune, blessings, and inheritance. Strong connections between the lords of these houses are traditionally considered the foundation of wealth in a chart.

What is Lakshmi Yoga?

Lakshmi Yoga is a wealth yoga named after Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of abundance. Classical texts describe it as forming when the 9th lord is placed in a Kendra (1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th house) in its own sign or exaltation, while the Lagna lord is also strong. An alternate formation involves Venus in its own sign or exalted in a Kendra or Trikona. It is traditionally associated with lasting prosperity and material comfort. What are Daridra Yogas and how do I mitigate them?

Daridra Yogas are combinations classical texts describe as obstructing wealth — for example, the 11th lord placed in the 6th, 8th, or 12th house, or the 2nd lord afflicted by malefics. They are not absolute and can be mitigated by other strong combinations in the chart. Traditional remedies include charity (especially during the Dasha of the afflicted planet), specific mantras, and disciplined financial habits. These are spiritual practices, not financial guarantees.
