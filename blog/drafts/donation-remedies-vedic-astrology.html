Donation Remedies (Daan) for All 9 Planets

In Vedic astrology, Daan (donation) is traditionally considered one of the most accessible and widely recommended remedies for pacifying weak or afflicted planets in a birth chart. The practice is rooted in the belief that giving specific items associated with a planet can help reduce its negative influence and strengthen its positive qualities.

What Is Daan in Vedic Astrology?

Daan, or charitable donation, is a traditional Vedic remedy that involves giving specific items to those in need — typically on the day associated with a particular planet. The underlying idea is that by donating items linked to a planet's energy, one may help balance that planet's influence in their chart.

According to classical texts, Daan is believed to be most effective when performed with sincerity, without expectation of return, and on the appropriate day. It is often recommended alongside other remedies such as mantras, gemstones, and fasting.

Sun (Surya)

The Sun is traditionally associated with authority, vitality, and the father. When the Sun is considered weak or afflicted in a chart, the following donations are commonly suggested:

  • Wheat — often donated on Sundays
  • Jaggery (gur) — a traditional sweetener linked to solar energy
  • Copper — the metal traditionally associated with the Sun

Moon (Chandra)

The Moon is traditionally linked to the mind, emotions, and the mother. Donation remedies for the Moon typically include:

  • Rice — often donated on Mondays
  • Milk — or milk-based items, associated with lunar calm
  • Silver — the metal traditionally connected to the Moon

Mars (Mangal)

Mars is traditionally associated with courage, energy, and siblings. When Mars is considered afflicted, these donations are commonly recommended:

  • Red lentils (masoor dal) — often donated on Tuesdays
  • Copper — items or vessels made of copper
  • Weapons or sharp instruments — traditionally linked to Mars's warrior energy

Mercury (Budh)

Mercury is traditionally linked to intellect, communication, and commerce. Suggested donation items for Mercury include:

  • Green moong dal — often donated on Wednesdays
  • Emerald-coloured items — green-hued articles associated with Mercury
  • Books or stationery — reflecting Mercury's connection to learning

Jupiter (Guru)

Jupiter is traditionally considered the planet of wisdom, fortune, and spirituality. Common donation remedies for Jupiter include:

  • Yellow items — such as yellow cloth, bananas, or chana dal, often donated on Thursdays
  • Turmeric (haldi) — strongly associated with Jupiter's energy
  • Gold — the metal traditionally connected to Jupiter

Venus (Shukra)

Venus is traditionally linked to love, beauty, and material comforts. Donation remedies for Venus typically include:

  • White items — such as white cloth, sugar, or camphor, often donated on Fridays
  • Rice — white rice or rice flour
  • Silver — the metal traditionally associated with Venus

Saturn (Shani)

Saturn is traditionally associated with discipline, karma, and longevity. When Saturn is considered afflicted, these donations are commonly suggested:

  • Black sesame seeds (til) — often donated on Saturdays
  • Mustard oil — a traditional Saturn remedy item
  • Iron — the metal traditionally connected to Saturn

Rahu

Rahu is a shadow planet traditionally linked to illusion, obsession, and unconventional paths. Donation remedies for Rahu may include:

  • Blue or dark-coloured items — such as blue cloth or blankets
  • Electrical goods — reflecting Rahu's association with technology and modern devices

Ketu

Ketu is a shadow planet traditionally associated with spirituality, detachment, and past-life karma. Common donation items for Ketu include:

  • Brown or earthy-coloured items — such as brown cloth or sesame
  • Blankets — often donated to the needy, traditionally associated with Ketu's energy

When & How to Donate

According to traditional guidelines, Daan remedies are believed to be most effective when certain conditions are observed:

  • Day of donation: Each planet is associated with a specific weekday — Sunday (Sun), Monday (Moon), Tuesday (Mars), Wednesday (Mercury), Thursday (Jupiter), Friday (Venus), and Saturday (Saturn). Rahu and Ketu donations are often suggested on Saturdays or during eclipses.
  • Time: Donations are traditionally recommended during the morning hours, ideally before noon.
  • Recipient: Items are typically given to Brahmins, the needy, or at temples, depending on the tradition followed.
  • Intention: The donation is traditionally believed to be more effective when performed with genuine sincerity and without expectation of return.

Important Notes

  • Daan remedies are based on traditional Vedic astrology beliefs and should not be considered a substitute for professional advice in any area of life.
  • The effectiveness of any remedy is a matter of personal belief and tradition. Different astrologers may recommend different items or approaches.
  • It is generally advised to consult a knowledgeable astrologer before performing specific remedies, as the appropriate remedy depends on the individual chart.
  • Donation remedies are traditionally seen as one part of a broader approach that may also include mantras, gemstones, fasting, and other practices.
