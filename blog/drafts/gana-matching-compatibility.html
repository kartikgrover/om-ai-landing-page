Gana Matching in Kundli: Deva, Manushya & Rakshasa

In the Ashtakoot system of Kundli matching, Gana is one of the eight factors used to assess compatibility between two individuals. It is traditionally believed to reflect a person's temperament and general disposition, which can influence how two people relate to each other in daily life.

What Is Gana in Vedic Astrology?

Gana refers to a classification of temperament assigned to each of the 27 Nakshatras (lunar mansions) in Vedic astrology. Every person's birth Nakshatra falls into one of three Gana categories, and this classification is traditionally used to gauge basic temperamental compatibility between partners.

In the Ashtakoot matching system, Gana carries a maximum of 6 points out of the total 36, making it one of the more heavily weighted factors in the compatibility assessment.

The Three Ganas

Each Nakshatra is assigned to one of the following three Ganas:

  • Deva (Divine): Traditionally associated with a gentle, compassionate, and accommodating nature. People with Deva Gana are often described as soft-spoken, spiritual-minded, and inclined toward harmony.
  • Manushya (Human): Traditionally associated with a balanced, practical, and adaptable temperament. Manushya Gana individuals are often described as grounded, with a mix of both gentle and assertive qualities.
  • Rakshasa (Fierce): Traditionally associated with an independent, strong-willed, and direct nature. Despite the name, Rakshasa Gana does not imply negativity — it is generally understood to reflect self-reliance, decisiveness, and a straightforward approach to life.

How Gana Matching Works

In Gana matching, the Ganas of both partners are compared and a score is assigned based on their combination. The traditional scoring is as follows:

  • Same Gana (e.g., Deva + Deva): 6 points — considered the most compatible pairing.
  • Deva + Manushya: 5 points — traditionally seen as a generally harmonious match.
  • Manushya + Rakshasa: 1 point — considered a less compatible pairing in traditional interpretation.
  • Deva + Rakshasa: 0 points — traditionally viewed as the most challenging combination due to differing temperamental tendencies.

It is worth noting that different traditions and texts may present slightly different scoring tables, and some astrologers may interpret these combinations with additional nuance.

What a Low Gana Score Means

A low Gana score is traditionally interpreted as indicating potential differences in temperament and approach to life. For example, a Deva-Rakshasa pairing might suggest that one partner tends toward gentleness while the other leans toward directness, which could lead to friction in some situations.

However, it is important to keep in mind that Gana is just one of eight factors in the Ashtakoot system. A low Gana score does not necessarily mean the overall match is unfavorable — the total score across all eight factors provides a more complete picture. Many astrologers also consider additional chart factors beyond the Ashtakoot system when assessing compatibility.

Beyond the Score

While Gana matching offers a useful lens for thinking about temperamental compatibility, it is a simplified framework. Real-world relationships involve many dimensions that a single score cannot fully capture. Some astrologers recommend looking at the full birth charts of both individuals — including planetary placements, house overlays, and dasha periods — for a more comprehensive understanding of compatibility.

Ultimately, Gana matching is best understood as one traditional tool among many, offering a starting point for reflection rather than a definitive verdict on a relationship's potential.
