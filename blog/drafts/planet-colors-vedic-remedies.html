Planet Colors in Vedic Astrology: What to Wear & When

In Vedic astrology, each of the nine planets (Navagraha) is traditionally associated with specific colors. Wearing these colors on the corresponding day of the week is one of the simplest remedies believed to help strengthen a planet's positive influence in your chart.

Colors & Planets in Vedic Astrology

The connection between planets and colors has roots in classical Jyotish texts. Each planet is said to radiate a particular energy, and colors are traditionally seen as a way to resonate with or amplify that energy. This is why wearing certain colors on specific weekdays is a commonly recommended remedy in Vedic astrology traditions.

It is worth noting that these associations may vary slightly across different schools of Vedic astrology, though the core correspondences tend to be consistent.

Color for Each Planet

Here are the colors traditionally associated with each of the nine planets:

  • Sun (Surya): Red and Orange — Sunday is the Sun's day. Wearing red or orange on Sundays is traditionally believed to strengthen the Sun's influence, which is associated with confidence, authority, and vitality.
  • Moon (Chandra): White and Silver — Monday is the Moon's day. White or silver tones are said to support emotional balance and mental calm, qualities traditionally linked to the Moon.
  • Mars (Mangal): Red and Coral — Tuesday belongs to Mars. Shades of red and coral are traditionally associated with courage, energy, and physical strength.
  • Mercury (Budh): Green — Wednesday is Mercury's day. Green is believed to support communication, intellect, and analytical thinking, areas traditionally governed by Mercury.
  • Jupiter (Guru): Yellow and Gold — Thursday is Jupiter's day. Yellow and golden hues are said to resonate with wisdom, prosperity, and spiritual growth.
  • Venus (Shukra): White and Pink — Friday belongs to Venus. White and pink are traditionally linked to beauty, harmony, love, and artistic expression.
  • Saturn (Shani): Black and Dark Blue — Saturday is Saturn's day. Dark colors are traditionally associated with discipline, patience, and perseverance.
  • Rahu: Blue and Smoky shades — Rahu does not rule a specific weekday but is sometimes associated with Saturday. Smoky blue and grey-blue tones are traditionally linked to this shadow planet.
  • Ketu: Brown and Grey — Like Rahu, Ketu does not have a dedicated weekday. Earthy tones such as brown and grey are traditionally associated with Ketu's energy of detachment and spirituality.

How to Use Colors as Remedies

Color-based remedies are considered among the most accessible in Vedic astrology. Here are some common ways they are traditionally applied:

  • Clothing: Wearing the planet's color on its corresponding weekday is the most common approach. Even a small accessory like a scarf, tie, or handkerchief in the right color may be considered sufficient.
  • Home and surroundings: Some traditions suggest incorporating planetary colors into your living space, particularly in areas where you spend significant time.
  • Focus on weak planets: Color remedies are often recommended specifically for planets that appear weak or afflicted in a birth chart, rather than for all planets at once.
  • Consistency: As with most traditional remedies, regular and consistent practice is generally considered more beneficial than occasional use.

Important Notes

  • Color remedies are part of a broader system of Vedic astrological remedies and are generally considered supportive rather than standalone solutions.
  • The effectiveness of color remedies is a matter of traditional belief and personal experience. They are not scientifically validated.
  • Different astrologers may recommend slightly different shades or approaches depending on their tradition and interpretation of your chart.
  • It is generally advisable to consult a knowledgeable astrologer to understand which planets in your chart may benefit most from color-based remedies.
