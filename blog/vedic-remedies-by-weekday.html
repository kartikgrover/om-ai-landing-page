Vedic Remedies by Weekday: A Simple Daily Guide

In Vedic astrology, each day of the week is traditionally associated with a specific planet. Practitioners have long believed that aligning simple daily practices — such as wearing certain colors, reciting mantras, or making donations — with the ruling planet of the day can help strengthen that planet's positive influence in one's life.

Planets & Weekdays

The seven days of the week are each governed by one of the seven visible celestial bodies recognized in Vedic astrology. This correspondence is believed to date back thousands of years and forms the basis for many traditional remedy practices:

  • Sunday — Sun (Surya)
  • Monday — Moon (Chandra)
  • Tuesday — Mars (Mangal)
  • Wednesday — Mercury (Budh)
  • Thursday — Jupiter (Guru/Brihaspati)
  • Friday — Venus (Shukra)
  • Saturday — Saturn (Shani)

By observing simple remedies on the appropriate day, one is traditionally recommended to honor and strengthen the corresponding planet's energy in their birth chart.

Sunday (Sun)

Sunday is ruled by the Sun (Surya), which is traditionally associated with vitality, confidence, authority, and the soul. Remedies on this day are believed to help strengthen one's sense of self and leadership qualities.

  • Surya Namaskar: Performing the Sun Salutation yoga sequence at sunrise is traditionally recommended as one of the most effective Sunday remedies.
  • Wear red or orange: These colors are believed to resonate with the Sun's energy.
  • Offer water to the Sun: Pouring water from a copper vessel while facing the rising sun is a widely practiced tradition.

Monday (Moon)

Monday is governed by the Moon (Chandra), which is traditionally linked to emotions, the mind, nurturing, and mental peace. Monday remedies are believed to promote calmness and emotional balance.

  • Wear white clothes: White is traditionally associated with the Moon's cool, calming energy.
  • Milk donation: Donating milk or offering it at a temple is believed to please the Moon.
  • Shiva worship: Monday is traditionally considered auspicious for Lord Shiva, who is believed to carry the Moon on his head.

Tuesday (Mars)

Tuesday is ruled by Mars (Mangal), which is traditionally associated with courage, energy, and determination. Tuesday remedies are believed to help channel Mars's fiery energy constructively.

  • Hanuman Chalisa: Reciting the Hanuman Chalisa is traditionally recommended on Tuesdays, as Lord Hanuman is believed to be closely connected with Mars.
  • Red lentils (masoor dal): Donating red lentils is a commonly suggested Mars remedy.
  • Wear red: Red clothing on Tuesdays is believed to honor Mars's energy.

Wednesday (Mercury)

Wednesday is governed by Mercury (Budh), which is traditionally linked to communication, intellect, and business acumen. Wednesday remedies are believed to sharpen the mind and improve speech.

  • Wear green: Green clothing is traditionally associated with Mercury's energy.
  • Feed birds: Offering green moong dal or grains to birds is a commonly recommended Mercury remedy.
  • Study or learn: Engaging in intellectual pursuits on Wednesdays is believed to align with Mercury's scholarly nature.

Thursday (Jupiter)

Thursday is ruled by Jupiter (Guru or Brihaspati), traditionally considered the most benefic planet. Jupiter is associated with wisdom, spirituality, and good fortune. Thursday remedies are believed to attract blessings and expand one's knowledge.

  • Wear yellow: Yellow clothing and accessories are traditionally recommended to honor Jupiter.
  • Help elders: Serving and respecting elders, teachers, and gurus is believed to strengthen Jupiter's influence.
  • Visit a temple: Thursday is traditionally considered an auspicious day for prayer and spiritual learning.

Friday (Venus)

Friday is governed by Venus (Shukra), which is traditionally associated with love, beauty, comfort, and the arts. Friday remedies are believed to enhance harmony and attract positive relationships.

  • White flowers: Offering or donating white flowers is a traditionally recommended Venus remedy.
  • Sweets donation: Distributing sweets or sugar to those in need is believed to please Venus.
  • Wear white or pastel colors: Light, elegant clothing is traditionally associated with Venus's refined energy.

Saturday (Saturn)

Saturday is ruled by Saturn (Shani), which is traditionally associated with discipline, karma, hard work, and justice. Saturn remedies are believed to help reduce hardships and earn the planet's favor through selfless service.

  • Help the poor: Acts of charity and service to the underprivileged are traditionally considered the most powerful Saturn remedy.
  • Black sesame (til): Donating black sesame seeds or mustard oil is a widely recommended practice.
  • Wear dark colors: Black or dark blue clothing is traditionally associated with Saturn's energy.

Tips for Practice

If you are new to Vedic remedies, here are a few practical suggestions traditionally recommended by practitioners:

  • Start small: You do not need to follow every remedy. Begin with the day associated with a planet that is believed to need strengthening in your chart.
  • Consistency matters: Practicing a remedy regularly on its designated day is traditionally considered more effective than occasional observance.
  • Intention is key: Remedies are believed to work best when performed with sincerity and a calm mind, rather than out of fear.
  • Combine with mantras: Reciting the beej (seed) mantra of the ruling planet is traditionally recommended alongside physical remedies.
  • Consult your chart: For best results, it is generally advised to understand which planets need attention in your specific birth chart before committing to a routine.
