9 Planets (Navagraha) in Vedic Astrology — Roles & Significance

Guide · February 20, 2026 · 5 min read

Introduction

The Navagraha — the nine planets — form the core of Vedic astrology. They include seven visible celestial bodies (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, and Saturn) plus two shadow planets (Rahu and Ketu), which are the north and south lunar nodes. Each planet carries specific characteristics, rules particular zodiac signs, and produces distinct effects depending on its placement, strength, and associations in your birth chart.

The Nine Planets

1. Sun (Surya)

The Sun represents the soul, ego, father, authority, government, and vitality. It is the king among planets and signifies leadership, self-confidence, and one's core identity. The Sun rules Leo and is exalted in Aries. A strong Sun bestows authority, recognition, and a robust constitution, while a weak or afflicted Sun may bring issues with self-esteem, health, or conflicts with authority figures.

2. Moon (Chandra)

The Moon governs the mind, emotions, mother, nurturing instincts, and public image. It is the queen of the planetary cabinet and reflects your emotional nature and mental well-being. The Moon rules Cancer and is exalted in Taurus. A well-placed Moon grants emotional stability, a caring nature, and popularity, while an afflicted Moon can lead to anxiety, mood swings, and mental restlessness.

3. Mars (Mangal)

Mars embodies energy, courage, siblings, property, and aggression. It is the commander-in-chief, representing drive, ambition, and physical strength. Mars rules Aries and Scorpio and is exalted in Capricorn. A strong Mars gives determination and bravery, while an afflicted Mars can cause anger, accidents, conflicts, and the well-known Manglik Dosha in matters of marriage.

4. Mercury (Budha)

Mercury signifies intellect, communication, commerce, and adaptability. It is the prince among planets, youthful and curious by nature. Mercury rules Gemini and Virgo and is exalted in Virgo. A strong Mercury blesses one with sharp intelligence, eloquence, and business acumen, while a weak Mercury may result in communication difficulties, indecisiveness, or nervous disorders.

5. Jupiter (Guru / Brihaspati)

Jupiter represents wisdom, spirituality, children, wealth, and expansion. Known as the great benefic and the guru of the gods, Jupiter is the most auspicious planet in Vedic astrology. It rules Sagittarius and Pisces and is exalted in Cancer. A well-placed Jupiter brings knowledge, prosperity, good fortune, and spiritual inclination, while a weak Jupiter may lead to poor judgment, financial difficulties, or lack of faith.

6. Venus (Shukra)

Venus governs love, beauty, luxury, marriage, and creativity. It is the guru of the demons and represents all forms of pleasure, art, and refinement. Venus rules Taurus and Libra and is exalted in Pisces. A strong Venus brings harmonious relationships, artistic talent, and material comforts, while an afflicted Venus can cause issues in love, overindulgence, or lack of aesthetic sensibility.

7. Saturn (Shani)

Saturn represents discipline, karma, delays, hard work, and longevity. Often feared but deeply significant, Saturn is the planet of justice and teaches through restriction and perseverance. Saturn rules Capricorn and Aquarius and is exalted in Libra. A well-placed Saturn grants discipline, endurance, and long-term success, while an afflicted Saturn can bring chronic struggles, delays, and periods of hardship such as Sade Sati.

8. Rahu (North Node)

Rahu is a shadow planet representing obsession, materialism, foreign things, unconventional paths, and illusion. It has no physical body but is the north lunar node — the point where the Moon's orbit crosses the ecliptic. Rahu has no sign rulership in the traditional scheme but amplifies the energy of whatever sign and house it occupies. It drives ambition, innovation, and worldly desires, but can also create confusion, deception, and insatiable cravings when poorly placed.

9. Ketu (South Node)

Ketu is the other shadow planet, representing detachment, spirituality, past-life karma, mysticism, and liberation. As the south lunar node, Ketu is the tail of the celestial serpent and signifies what we have already mastered in past lives. It has no traditional sign rulership. A well-placed Ketu grants spiritual insight, intuition, and liberation from material bonds, while an afflicted Ketu can cause aimlessness, sudden losses, or mysterious health issues.

Benefic vs Malefic Planets

In Vedic astrology, planets are classified as natural benefics or natural malefics. Jupiter, Venus, the waxing Moon, and a well-associated Mercury are considered natural benefics — they tend to produce favorable results. Saturn, Mars, Rahu, Ketu, and the Sun are considered natural malefics — they bring challenges, though these challenges often lead to growth and resilience.

It is important to note that functional benefic or malefic status depends on the ascendant (Lagna). A natural malefic like Saturn can become a highly beneficial planet for certain ascendants (such as Taurus or Libra), while a natural benefic like Jupiter can act as a functional malefic for others. This distinction is crucial for accurate chart interpretation.

Planetary Periods (Dasha)

The Vimshottari Dasha system is the most widely used timing technique in Vedic astrology. It assigns specific periods to each of the nine planets over a 120-year cycle. During a planet's dasha (major period) or antardasha (sub-period), the themes associated with that planet become especially prominent in your life. For example, during Jupiter's dasha, you may experience growth in wisdom, children, or wealth, while Saturn's dasha often brings lessons through discipline and hard work. Understanding your current dasha is essential for predicting life events and timing important decisions.
