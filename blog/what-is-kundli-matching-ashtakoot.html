Kundli Matching & Ashtakoot Guide: How Compatibility Works

Table of Contents

  • The 8 Koots Explained
  • How to Interpret Scores
  • Beyond the Score

Kundli matching (also called Kundali Milan or horoscope matching) is a traditional Vedic practice used to assess marriage compatibility. The most popular method is Ashtakoot Milan — an 8-point matching system that scores compatibility out of 36 points.

The 8 Koots Explained

# Koot Points What It Measures
1 Varna 1 Spiritual temperament and work-nature compatibility. Based on classical categories that reflect innate qualities, not social hierarchy.
2 Vashya 2 Mutual attraction and influence between partners.
3 Tara 3 Birth star compatibility, health and well-being of the couple.
4 Yoni 4 Physical and sexual compatibility, based on animal symbols of nakshatras.
5 Graha Maitri 5 Mental compatibility based on Moon sign lords' friendship.
6 Gana 6 Temperament matching — Deva (calm, spiritual), Manushya (practical, balanced), Rakshasa (independent, intense). These are personality archetypes, not moral labels.
7 Bhakoot 7 Emotional compatibility, wealth and health prospects of the couple.
8 Nadi 8 Constitutional and health compatibility (traditionally assessed). Carries the highest weight. Same Nadi = Nadi Dosha.

How to Interpret Scores

  • Below 12: Below average — significant challenges likely.
  • 12-17: Average compatibility, remedies recommended.
  • 18-23: Good compatibility.
  • 24-27: Very good match.
  • 28 and above: Excellent match.

Beyond the Score

Ashtakoot is important but not the only factor. A good astrologer also checks:

  • Manglik Dosha
  • Planetary strengths
  • Dasha compatibility
  • Navamsa chart
