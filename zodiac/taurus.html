Taurus — Vrishabha (वृषभ)

The second sign of the Vedic zodiac. Grounded, sensual, and blessed by Venus — Vrishabha rashi natives are the steady builders who create lasting wealth and beauty.

Vrishabha Rashi: Personality & Traits

In Vedic astrology, Taurus or Vrishabha is the second rashi of the zodiac, ruled by Venus (Shukra) — the planet of beauty, love, luxury, and material comfort. Vrishabha natives are the solid foundation upon which lasting things are built. They possess remarkable patience, determination, and an innate understanding of material value. Where Aries rushes forward, Taurus stands firm and builds deliberately.

The fixed (Sthira) quality of Vrishabha makes these natives incredibly persistent. Once they set a course, very little can divert them. This steadfastness is both their greatest strength and their potential weakness — it manifests as reliability and loyalty but can also become stubbornness and resistance to change. The three Nakshatras within Vrishabha each add unique dimensions: Krittika (padas 2-4) brings the sharp, purifying fire of the Sun; Rohini — considered the most fertile and creative Nakshatra — gives artistic talent and magnetic charm; Mrigashira (padas 1-2) adds curiosity, searching nature, and intellectual restlessness.

Venus' rulership gives Vrishabha natives a deep appreciation for beauty in all forms — music, art, fine food, comfortable living spaces, and harmonious relationships. They have a natural eye for aesthetics and often gravitate toward creative fields. Their relationship with material wealth is nuanced: they are not merely acquisitive but understand the value of security and comfort as a foundation for a well-lived life.

Physically, Vrishabha natives tend to have a solid, well-proportioned build with pleasant facial features. Venus blesses them with an attractive appearance and a soothing voice — many accomplished singers have strong Taurus placements. Their constitution is generally robust, though they may tend toward weight gain due to their love of good food and comfort.

Venus (Shukra) — The Ruler of Taurus

Venus holds the position of minister among the Navagraha in Vedic astrology. As the lord of Vrishabha rashi, Venus bestows charm, artistic sensibility, financial intelligence, and the capacity for deep love. The placement of Venus in the birth chart is paramount for Taurus natives — a well-placed Venus in a Kendra or Trikona, or in its exaltation sign Pisces, amplifies wealth, beauty, and harmonious relationships.

When Venus is strong in the Kundli, Taurus natives excel in accumulating wealth, creating beauty, and maintaining loving partnerships. They become natural aesthetes, diplomats, and financial advisors. However, an afflicted Venus — combusted by the Sun, conjunct malefics like Saturn or Rahu, or debilitated in Virgo — can create issues with overindulgence, relationship difficulties, and financial mismanagement. Classical texts recommend Diamond (Heera) or White Sapphire and the chanting of Shukra mantras as remedies for Venus-related challenges.

Career & Finance for Taurus

Vrishabha rashi natives thrive in careers that combine stability with aesthetic sensibility. Banking, finance, real estate, agriculture, hospitality, fashion, interior design, music, and culinary arts are all excellent fits. Venus gives them a natural understanding of value — what things are worth, how to create beauty, and how to grow wealth steadily. They make excellent long-term investors, preferring safe, appreciating assets over speculative ventures.

Financially, Taurus natives are among the most capable wealth builders in the zodiac. Their patience allows them to think in decades rather than days. The 2nd house (wealth) from Taurus is Gemini, ruled by Mercury, indicating that communication skills, intellectual pursuits, and multiple income streams contribute to their financial growth. The Dasha periods of Mercury, Saturn, and Venus tend to be the most prosperous for Vrishabha rashi natives, bringing steady accumulation and material security.

Love & Relationships

In love, Taurus natives are devoted, sensual, and deeply loyal partners. They seek stability and long-term commitment over fleeting romance. The 7th house (partnerships) from Taurus is Scorpio, ruled by Mars, indicating that Taurus natives are attracted to intense, passionate, and emotionally deep partners — a fascinating dynamic between Venus' gentleness and Mars' intensity.

Compatibility in Vedic astrology shows that Taurus pairs exceptionally well with fellow earth signs Virgo (Kanya) and Capricorn (Makara), where shared pragmatism and values create a stable foundation. Cancer (Karka) and Pisces (Meena) also form harmonious bonds — water nourishes earth. Relationships with Leo (Simha) and Aquarius (Kumbha) can be more challenging due to the square aspect. Taurus natives should be mindful of possessiveness — their deep attachment to loved ones can sometimes become controlling if Venus is afflicted.

Health Areas to Watch

Taurus rules the throat, neck, thyroid gland, and lower jaw in medical astrology. Vrishabha natives may be prone to throat infections, thyroid disorders, tonsillitis, and neck stiffness. Venus' influence creates a Kapha-dominant constitution in Ayurveda, making them susceptible to weight gain, diabetes, and sluggish metabolism when their love of comfort overrides physical activity. Regular exercise — particularly walking and yoga — is important for Taurus natives. They benefit from warm, stimulating foods and should moderate their intake of sweets and heavy dairy products.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Taurus (Vrishabha) in Vedic astrology?

Taurus, known as Vrishabha (वृषभ) in Vedic astrology, is the second sign of the zodiac. It is an earth sign ruled by Venus (Shukra). Vrishabha rashi spans from 30 to 60 degrees of the sidereal zodiac and contains the Nakshatras Krittika (padas 2-4), Rohini, and Mrigashira (padas 1-2). Natives are known for their stability, sensuality, and material wisdom.

What are the lucky gems and colors for Taurus?

The primary lucky gemstone for Taurus (Vrishabha) natives is Diamond (Heera), which strengthens their ruling planet Venus. White Sapphire is an affordable alternative. Lucky colors include white, cream, and pastel shades. Friday is considered the most auspicious day.

Which signs are most compatible with Taurus in Vedic astrology?

In Vedic astrology, Taurus (Vrishabha) is most compatible with Virgo (Kanya) and Capricorn (Makara), as these fellow earth signs share Taurus' practical and grounded nature. Cancer (Karka) and Pisces (Meena) also form harmonious connections.

How does Venus influence Taurus natives?

Venus (Shukra) is the lord of Taurus and endows natives with a love of beauty, comfort, and luxury. A strong Venus gives artistic talent, charm, financial acumen, and harmonious relationships. When afflicted, it can lead to overindulgence, laziness, and attachment to material pleasures.
