What is Panchang?

Panchang (also spelled Panchangam) is the Hindu astrological almanac that tracks five key elements of each day: Tithi (lunar day), Nakshatra (lunar mansion), Yoga (luni-solar combination), Karana (half-tithi), and Vara (weekday). Together, these five elements — "pancha" meaning five and "anga" meaning limb — form the foundation of Vedic timekeeping and are used to determine auspicious timings (muhurat) for important activities like marriages, business ventures, travel, and religious ceremonies.

Why Check Daily Panchang?

Millions of people across India consult the Panchang daily to plan their activities. Knowing the Rahu Kalam helps you avoid starting important tasks during inauspicious periods. The Hora (planetary hour) system tells you which planet rules each hour of the day, helping you align your activities with favorable planetary energies — for example, Jupiter's hora is ideal for education and spiritual activities, while Venus's hora favors creative work and relationships.
