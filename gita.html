About the Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita ("Song of God") is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the epic Mahabharata. Set on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, it is a dialogue between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna, who serves as his charioteer. The Gita addresses the moral and philosophical dilemmas of life through teachings on duty (dharma), devotion (bhakti), knowledge (jnana), and selfless action (karma yoga). It is considered one of the most important texts in Hinduism and has been studied by spiritual seekers worldwide for over 2,000 years.

The 18 Chapters of the Bhagavad Gita

The Gita is divided into 18 chapters, each called a "Yoga" — a path to spiritual union. They begin with Arjuna's despair on the battlefield (Arjuna Vishada Yoga), progress through the paths of knowledge (Sankhya Yoga), action (Karma Yoga), and devotion (Bhakti Yoga), and culminate in the revelation of the universal form (Vishwarupa) and the final teaching on surrender and liberation (Moksha Sanyasa Yoga). Why Read a Daily Gita Verse?

Reading one verse daily is a time-honored practice that makes the Gita's vast wisdom accessible. Each shloka is complete in itself — a seed of insight you can contemplate throughout the day. Many practitioners find that a single verse, read with attention in the morning, provides clarity for decisions, comfort during difficulty, and a reminder of life's deeper purpose.
