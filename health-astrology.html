How AI Reads Your Health Chart

In Vedic astrology (Jyotish), health is read from a specific cluster of houses, planetary significators, and time periods. Om.AI examines all of them together — much like a traditional astrologer — and translates the patterns into a clear, structured reading.

6th House (Roga Bhava)

The house of disease, debts, and recovery. Reveals everyday illness patterns and how the body fights infection.

8th House (Randhra)

Linked to chronic conditions, surgery, accidents, and longevity. A central house in classical Ayur (life-span) calculations.

12th House (Vyaya)

Hospitalization, sleep, hidden ailments, and bed comforts. Read together with the 6th and 8th for the "Dusthana" picture.

Lagna & Lagna Lord

The 1st house and its lord describe overall vitality, body strength, and resilience — the foundation of any health reading.

Sun & Moon

Sun signifies vitality, heart, and spine. Moon governs mind, stomach, and bodily fluids. Both anchor the chart's wellbeing.

Dasha & Transits

Vimshottari Dasha and current Saturn / Mars / Rahu transits over malefic houses time the windows that need extra care.

What Each Planet Says About Health

Classical texts such as Phaladeepika and Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra link each planet to specific organs, humors (vata, pitta, kapha) and patterns of imbalance.

Sun — Vitality, Heart, Spine

A strong, well-placed Sun supports stamina, heart health, eyesight, and the spine. When weak or afflicted, classical texts describe bilious tendencies, low energy, eye strain, and cardiac sensitivity.

Moon — Mind, Fluids, Stomach

The Moon governs emotions, sleep, hydration, and the stomach. An afflicted Moon — especially associated with Saturn or Rahu — is traditionally read as a vulnerability to anxiety, low mood, water retention, and digestive sensitivity.

Mars — Blood, Inflammation, Accidents

Mars rules blood, muscles, surgical events, fevers, and inflammatory conditions. Difficult Mars influences on the 1st, 6th, or 8th houses are read as windows for caution around accidents, blood pressure spikes, and acute flare-ups.

Saturn — Chronic, Bones, Nerves

Saturn signifies long-term conditions, joints, bones, teeth, nerves, and wind-related (vata) imbalances. Saturn periods often coincide with structural or chronic concerns and benefit from steady routines, rest, and discipline.

Rahu — Mysterious & Modern Ailments

Rahu is linked to hard-to-diagnose conditions, allergies, addictions, and modern-lifestyle issues. Strong Rahu influence on the 6th or 8th house indicates a need for thorough check-ups rather than self-diagnosis.

Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Ketu

Mercury rules nerves and skin; Jupiter the liver and metabolic processes; Venus the reproductive system and kidneys; Ketu mysterious or psychosomatic patterns. Each adds nuance — Om.AI weighs them all.

Supportive Vedic Practices (Not Medical Treatment)

Classical Vedic and Lal Kitab texts describe simple practices for difficult planetary periods. They are framed as discipline, mindfulness, and gratitude — bringing focus and calm during stressful times. Treat them as complementary, never as a cure.

Mantras & Stotras

Mahamrityunjaya Mantra is traditionally chanted for general wellbeing and resilience. Hanuman Chalisa is associated with strength during Saturn or Mars-influenced periods. Use them as a daily focus practice.

Gemstones

Gemstones are highly chart-specific in Vedic astrology — the wrong stone can amplify the wrong energy. Never wear one based on a general reading. Have your chart reviewed first; always choose health and safety over any astrological advice.

Charity (Daan) & Service

Each planet has a traditional charity — feeding people on Saturday for Saturn, donating to the elderly, helping animals. The framework reframes a difficult period as an opportunity for kindness and perspective.

Routine & Lifestyle

The most universally helpful "remedy" texts repeat is also the most modern: regular sleep, balanced food, gentle exercise, and stress management. Saturn periods especially reward steady, simple routines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is health astrology and how does it work?

Health astrology — sometimes called medical astrology — studies the influence of houses, planets, and time periods on physical and mental wellbeing in your birth chart. Vedic astrology assigns the 6th house to disease and recovery, the 8th house to chronic conditions and longevity, and the 12th house to hospitalization and sleep. Each planet rules specific organs and tendencies. Om.AI reads these factors together to flag periods when health may need extra care. It is educational guidance, not medical advice.

Which houses in Vedic astrology indicate disease and health?

Three houses are central. The 6th house (Roga Bhava) governs everyday illness, infections, debts, and recovery. The 8th house (Randhra Bhava) covers chronic disease, surgery, accidents, and longevity. The 12th house (Vyaya Bhava) signifies hospitalization, sleep disturbance, and hidden conditions. The 1st house (Lagna) reflects overall vitality, while the Moon represents mind and the Sun represents core vitality. Om.AI examines the lords, occupants, and aspects of all of these in your chart.

What do Mars, Saturn, and Rahu indicate about health?

Classical texts associate Mars with blood, inflammation, fevers, accidents, and surgical events. Saturn is linked to chronic and slow-developing conditions, bones, joints, teeth, nerves, and wind-related ailments. Rahu is connected to mysterious, hard-to-diagnose, or modern lifestyle conditions. When these planets influence the 1st, 6th, 8th, or 12th houses — or run Dashas activating those houses — the chart suggests a period for extra preventive care. This is a traditional framework, not a diagnosis.

Can astrology predict the timing of health issues?

Vedic astrology can identify windows when health stress is more likely — typically during the Dasha or Antardasha of malefic planets affecting the 6th, 8th, or 12th house, or during difficult transits of Saturn, Mars, or Rahu over those houses. Om.AI maps your Vimshottari Dasha timeline and flags periods that classical texts associate with health vulnerability. These are guidance windows for preventive care — not predictions of specific illness.

Does astrology cover mental health?

In Vedic astrology, the Moon represents the mind, emotions, and mental stability. A weak or afflicted Moon — especially when associated with Saturn, Rahu, Ketu, or the 6th, 8th, or 12th houses — is traditionally read as a vulnerability to anxiety, low mood, or mental fatigue. The 5th house also reflects mental peace. Om.AI can describe these patterns in your chart, but mental health is medical and personal. Please consult a qualified mental health professional for clinical concerns.

Are astrological remedies a substitute for medical treatment?

No. Mantras, gemstones, charity, and rituals are part of the cultural and spiritual tradition of Vedic astrology and are presented as supportive practices to bring focus, discipline, and peace of mind. They are not medical treatment. Never delay, replace, or discontinue medical care because of an astrological reading. If you are unwell, consult a qualified healthcare professional first; spiritual practices can complement, but never replace, medical advice.

How does AI read a health chart compared to a traditional astrologer?

Om.AI calculates your full birth chart, Navamsa, Dashas, and current transits, then applies classical rules from Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra, Phaladeepika, and Uttara Kalamrita to assess house lords, planetary dignities, and timing factors. It can generate a structured reading in seconds and answer follow-up questions. A traditional astrologer adds personal interpretation and intuition. Both approaches are educational; neither is medical advice.
