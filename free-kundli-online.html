What's in a Kundli

A Kundli (Janam Kundli) is the complete map of the sky at the moment you were born. It is the foundation of all Vedic astrology predictions.

D1 Rashi Chart

Your primary birth chart showing planets across the 12 zodiac signs and houses

9 Vedic Planets

Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu & Ketu (Navagraha)

12 Houses (Bhavas)

Each house governs a life area — career, marriage, wealth, health, education & more

27 Nakshatras

Lunar mansions that refine each planet's influence and determine Dasha periods

Ascendant (Lagna)

The rising sign at your birth — the lens through which your entire chart is read

Vimshottari Dasha

Planetary period timeline that reveals when key events will unfold in your life

In Vedic astrology, no two Kundlis are alike. Even twins born minutes apart can have different Ascendants and house cusps, leading to meaningfully different life paths. That is why your exact birth time and location matter — they determine the precise arrangement of planets that makes your chart uniquely yours.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Kundli?

A Kundli (also called Janam Kundli, birth chart, or natal chart) is a map of the sky at the exact moment you were born, as viewed from your birth location. It shows the positions of all 9 Vedic planets — Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, and Ketu — across the 12 houses of the zodiac. In Vedic astrology, your Kundli is the foundation for all predictions. It reveals personality traits, career potential, relationship patterns, health tendencies, and life timing through Dasha periods.

What information do I need to generate my Kundli?

You need three things: your date of birth, time of birth (as precise as possible), and place of birth. The birth time is especially critical because it determines your Ascendant (Lagna), which shifts roughly every two hours. Even a 15-minute difference can change house placements and alter predictions. If you do not know your exact birth time, check your birth certificate or hospital records.

What is the difference between the Rashi chart (D1) and Navamsa chart (D9)?

The Rashi chart (D1) is your primary birth chart showing where each planet was at the time of your birth. It covers all major life areas. The Navamsa chart (D9) is a divisional chart that provides deeper insight into marriage, partnerships, spiritual growth, and the true strength of planets. A planet strong in D1 but weak in D9 may not fully deliver its promised results. Experienced Vedic astrologers always read both charts together for a complete picture.

What are yogas and doshas in a Kundli?

Yogas are specific planetary combinations that indicate particular life outcomes. For example, Gaja Kesari Yoga (Jupiter-Moon conjunction or aspect) indicates wisdom and prosperity, while Raj Yoga indicates success, authority, and recognition. Doshas are planetary afflictions that may create challenges — Manglik Dosha (Mars in certain houses) can affect marriage compatibility, and Kaal Sarp Dosha (all planets hemmed between Rahu and Ketu) may cause recurring obstacles. What is Vimshottari Dasha and why does it matter?

Vimshottari Dasha is the most widely used timing system in Vedic astrology. It divides your life into planetary periods (Mahadasha) governed by each of the 9 planets, determined by your Moon's Nakshatra at birth. Each Mahadasha lasts a fixed number of years — for example, Saturn governs for 19 years and Venus for 20 years. These are further subdivided into sub-periods (Antardasha). The Dasha system is how Vedic astrologers predict when events are likely to occur. Om.AI calculates your complete Dasha timeline and uses it to answer timing questions.
